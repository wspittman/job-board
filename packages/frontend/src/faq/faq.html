Frequently Asked Questions

We've collected the answers to the questions we hear most so you can get back to finding your next role.

Do we really need another job board?

We think so! Traditional job boards prioritize employers' needs over a seamless experience for job seekers. This is why so many of them end up with cluttered interfaces and generic search functions. Better Job Board is different: we put the job seeker front and center. This focus leads to a streamlined, user-first experience with powerful, intuitive search and filtering tools. We're here to make finding the right job faster, simpler, and a whole lot better.

Got an idea to make this better?

We'd love to hear it! Send your thoughts to contact@betterjobboard.net. We read every message and use your feedback to shape our improvements. While we can't reply to every message personally, your input goes a long way in helping us build a better experience for all job seekers.

How To Use

I found a great job! How do I apply?

Click the "Apply" button when you find a job you're interested in, and we'll direct you to the employer's official application page. We never stand between you and the employer – you'll always apply directly through their preferred channel.

How do I save my search and come back later?

Save a bookmark to the page. Every time you change your search, the URL in your browser's address bar automatically updates, allowing the bookmark to save the search. Using the bookmark will re-run your search to show updated jobs.

How do I share a job with a friend?

Click the clipboard button next to the Apply button. This will copy a sharable URL to your clipboard. Anyone using the URL will be brought back to that specific job for as long as it is available.

About the Jobs

Where do you find all these jobs?

We pull job posts directly from employers, accessing their applicant tracking systems (ATS) through official APIs. This way, we ensure high-quality, up-to-date posts while our smart search surfaces the most relevant opportunities for you.

Why do you only have US-based jobs?

Because we are deliberately focused on the US job market. Most job seekers search within a single country's hiring market, and the US is the one we know best. Hiring practices, norms, policies, and even job titles can vary a lot from country to country. By limiting the board to US-based jobs, we can make the experience more accurate and useful than if we spread our effort across very different markets.

For the curious, you can see our more detailed reasoning in our blog post Why We're Focusing on US-Based Jobs.

How fresh are these jobs?

We refresh our job posts daily to ensure you're seeing the latest opportunities. With that level of freshness, you can apply while they're still hot!

How do I know these jobs are still up for grabs?

We update our job posts every day and remove any that are no longer accepting applications. If you see it here, it was verified as active within the last 24 hours! If you do find a job that has been taken down since our last update, the apply link won't work.

For Recruiters

How do I get my company's jobs listed here?

Use our self-serve tool to add your company in minutes — it's free and no account is needed. Visit Add Your Company and enter your ATS and company slug. Jobs appear within 24 hours.

Which applicant tracking systems do you support?

We currently support Greenhouse and Lever. If your company uses a different ATS, let us know and we'll consider adding support.

I'm hiring! Can I post my job here?

We don't accept direct job submissions, but if your company uses Greenhouse or Lever you can add your company to get your US-based jobs indexed automatically. It's free and takes about a minute.

Other

What data do you collect about me?

We're committed to a privacy-first job search.

  • We don't require an account.
  • We avoid standard analytics packages (eg. Google Analytics, Mixpanel, etc) and collect the minimum data needed to understand aggregate usage and keep the site reliable.
  • All non-aggregate data is deleted automatically after 90 days.

Specifically, here's what we collect:

  • We store two random ids, one in your browser (localStorage) and one in your current tab (sessionStorage). These IDs aren't tied to personal identities, and they don't follow you across devices or browsers.
  • When your browser makes requests to our servers (eg. by loading the site or making a search), it also sends these two IDs along.

We use this data to understand how people use the site, diagnose issues, and improve the experience.
